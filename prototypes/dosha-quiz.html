The Building Blocks

Vata, Pitta & Kapha

Every person, plant, season, and storm carries all three doshas in its own proportion. A river holds more kapha; a windswept summit, more vata; a wildfire, more pitta. In the body, they keep us moving, transforming, and holding steady — together.

Air + Space

Vata

Movement + change.

Vata is the force of movement and change — the heartbeat, the breath, the flow of thought and digestion, and every shift from one moment to the next. Vata people tend to be creative, quick, and full of energy. Out of balance, vata turns to dryness, restlessness, anxiety, and irregular digestion.

Fire + Water

Pitta

Transformation.

Pitta turns food into nourishment and what we see into ideas and emotions — the fire that transforms. Pitta types are focused, ambitious, sharp, and warm, and at their best they know how to play, take up space, and step away from the to-do list. Out of balance, pitta runs hot: inflammation, acidity, and irritability.

Earth + Water

Kapha

Cushioning + structure.

Kapha is the cushion that steadies us and the very structure of the body — the calm of a deep river or a stand of trees. Kapha people are strong, grounded, calm, and loyal. In excess, kapha becomes heaviness, lethargy, and congestion.
